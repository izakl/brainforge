# Framework release 0.8.0

- Date: 2026-07-07
- Manifest compatibility: `>= 1.0.0`

## Summary

This release codifies the inherited **`brain-checks`** CI gate as a governed,
inheritable standard, and blesses richer "superset" capability generators. A new
inherited policy — `04-policies/ci-checks-standard.md` — states the contract:

- `capabilities --check` (anti-drift) and `intent-gate` (no command without a
  capabilities row) are **always blocking**;
- `docs-mesh` (links / wikilinks / mermaid resolve, freshness parses) is
  **blocking by default**, with a narrow, honest escape hatch — a lane MAY
  soft-fail **one** check `continue-on-error` **only** for a pre-existing
  unresolvable it cannot fix in scope, and only with a `TODO(<lane>-docs-mesh)`
  and a `::warning::`. It may **never** fake-pass by repointing links or editing
  append-only logs (Contoso's `TODO(contoso-docs-mesh)` is the reference precedent);
- a **superset allowance**: a lane MAY ship a richer, system-wide generator
  instead of the stock `python -m brainfactory` generator, provided its CI still
  enforces the same three invariants (Northwind's `08-ops/generate-capabilities.ps1`
  is the reference superset).

A hub guard (`scripts/check-brain-factory.sh`) now asserts the template still
ships `.github/workflows/brain-checks.yml` with its two blocking invocations, so
the standard cannot be silently removed.

## Action for brains

1. Run `<prefix>-upgrade` (dry-run, then `--apply`) to inherit the new
   `04-policies/ci-checks-standard.md` standard.
2. Confirm the inherited `.github/workflows/brain-checks.yml` is present and in
   sync with the hub, and that `capabilities --check` and `intent-gate` remain
   **blocking**. Document any local wiring deviation rather than silently forking.
3. If this lane ships a **superset** generator instead of the stock one, confirm
   its CI still enforces all three invariants (PR-time capabilities drift-check,
   intent parity, docs-mesh). That is compliant via the superset allowance.

## Notes

- Additive change: no existing command, module, or manifest field is removed, and
  `core_modules` is unchanged (this is a policy, not a module).
- See ADR 0027 for the decision record (why `brain-checks` is the inherited CI
  standard and why the superset-generator allowance is blessed).
